About Me

My name is James, and I am a twenty something year old Web Developer and Designer from the countryside of Berkshire in UK. My passions outside of web development are photography and the great outdoors. I have started a small herd of Shetland Ponies with my partner, and whilst it is hard work it has been possibly the most rewarding thing I have done in my life.

I first came involved with websites and computer arts at the age of thirteen, and armed with a cracked version of Photoshop that likely came from Limewire, I began putting together Wordpress websites and phpBB boards for pocket money. I made a name for myself designing websites and various displays for gaming teams and was the "guy to come to" if you needed anything doing in Photoshop.

Academically, I studied Computer Sciences which laid a strong foundation down for me. I developed software in Visual Basic and Pascal, and a stock allocation program gained me a position as technical staff at a local manufacturing company. Here, I spent my time creating and fixing PCB boards and maintaining the network of computers in the office.

Mid 2017 I made the decision to start to further my knowledge of web development with the intention of moving into a full-time career. I was fortunate enough to be in 1% of original applicants picked for the Google/Udacity front-end scholarship, which is a six month scholarship covering all aspects of responsive web development, to more advanced JS frameworks like React.
